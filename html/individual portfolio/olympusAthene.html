Olympus Athene

September 2020 - May 2021
2D Design Ilustration 3D Modeling

Olympus Athene emerges as a personal project, marking my first venture into the realm of video games, encompassing both design and modeling.

The project originates from a simple game idea for which I had to create a cover, a part of the 2D Digital Design course in the Design and Development of Video Games program at URJC. Later, the concept evolves within the 3D Digital Design course, involving the modeling of a game environment and character.

Olympus is a fictional video game saga of the action-adventure genre, where Greek mythology plays a significant role in both narrative and design. Across different installments, a series of Chosen Ones (Ekleltós in Greek) uncover the truth behind classic Greek myths through regressions and by taking control of various characters from Greek mythology.

Olympus Athene is one of the games in this saga, focusing on the story of Athena, one of the twelve Olympian gods. Her story unfolds as The Chosen One completes various myths from Ancient Greek religion, which manifest in the form of missions for the player.

In each game, the main character will be an ordinary person who one day has an encounter with the god who has chosen them, asking for their help in uncovering their true story. To do so, the protagonist will travel to the Isle of the Gods, a place invisible to the human eye, and once inhabited by the gods and creatures of Greek mythology. Upon reaching the island, through a temple located on a mountain, The Chosen One will journey back to the golden age of the gods to discover the truth about the disappearance of the island's inhabitants.

Olympus Athene is the first game in the saga, where the goddess Athena chooses an ordinary girl named Elia (ελιά or eliá in Greek; translated as olive) as her chosen one. Elia will be compelled to uncover the truth about her goddess and, simultaneously, about herself.

Development Process

The development process begins in September 2020, with the initial conception of the game idea and a potential cover that represents its essence; and it concludes in May 2021, with both a scenario and a character modeled. Progress was halted between November and February since the project originated within an academic context for a course, and the decision to continue the idea was not made until the second course was undertaken.
